8.2 Operations with Complex Numbers

Addition and Subtraction of Complex Numbers

Formula: (a + jb) + (c + jd) = (a + c) + j(b + d)

Example:

Add (3 + j4) and (1 - j2).

Separate the real and imaginary parts: (3 + 1) + j(4 - 2) = 4 + j2.

Multiplication and Division in Polar Form

Formula (Multiplication in Polar Form): r1∠θ1 × r2∠θ2 = (r1 × r2)∠(θ1 + θ2)

Example:

Multiply (2∠30°) by (3∠45°).

Steps: Multiply magnitudes: 2 × 3 = 6; Add angles: 30° + 45° = 75°.

Result: 6∠75°.

Interactive Exercise:

Convert (4 + j3) and (5 - j2) to polar form, multiply, and convert back to rectangular form. This is valuable for analyzing AC signals in audio circuits.

Magnitude and Phase Angle Calculation

Formula: |Z| = √(a² + b²), θ = tan-1(b/a)

Example:

Find the magnitude and angle for 5 + j12.

Magnitude |Z| = √(5² + 12²) ≈ 13; Angle θ ≈ 67.4°.

Result: 13∠67.4°.

8.3 Impedance and Admittance Calculations

Impedance of Resistors, Capacitors, and Inductors

Impedance affects frequency response in audio electronics. Calculate impedance at different frequencies for accurate filtering and tuning.

Example:

Calculate impedance for a 10Ω resistor, 0.1F capacitor, and 1H inductor at 60 Hz.

Results: Resistor ZR = 10 Ω; Capacitor ZC = -j26.53 Ω; Inductor ZL = j376.99 Ω.

Series and Parallel Impedance

Example (Series):

Calculate total impedance for Z1 = 4 + j3 Ω and Z2 = 5 - j2 Ω.

Total: Ztotal = 9 + j1 Ω.

Example (Parallel):

Calculate total impedance for parallel branches Z1 = 4 + j3 Ω and Z2 = 6 - j2 Ω.

Total: Ztotal = (Z1-1 + Z2-1)-1.

8.4 Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL) and Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL) in AC Circuits

Applying KVL

Formula: ∑ V = 0

Example:

Apply KVL to an audio filter circuit to find voltage across each component.

Applying KCL

Formula: ∑ Iin = ∑ Iout

Example:

Use KCL to determine current flow in a parallel RLC audio circuit.

8.5 Power in AC Circuits

Calculating Real, Reactive, and Apparent Power

Formulas:

  • Real Power: P = VI cos θ
  • Reactive Power: Q = VI sin θ
  • Apparent Power: S = √(P² + Q²)

Example:

Given V = 100V, I = 5A, and θ = 30°, find P, Q, and S.

Results: P ≈ 433 W, Q ≈ 250 VAR, S ≈ 500 VA.

8.6 Resonance in AC Circuits

Series Resonance in Audio Electronics

Formula: fr = 1 / (2π√(LC))

Example:

Calculate resonant frequency for L = 10 mH and C = 100 μF.

Result: fr ≈ 159.15 Hz. Resonance can be used to fine-tune audio filters.

Interactive Tool:

Adjust values of L and C to observe resonance frequency changes, mimicking audio circuit tuning for specific frequency responses.

8.7 Practical Applications in Audio Electronics

Voltage Gain in Amplifiers

Example:

Calculate voltage gain for Vin = 1V and Vout = 10V.

Gain (dB): 20 dB.

Frequency Response of Audio Filters

Example:

Calculate the cutoff frequency for a low-pass RC filter with R = 10kΩ and C = 0.01 μF.

Formula: fc = 1 / (2πRC)

Solution: Convert values to base units: R = 10,000 Ω, C = 10-8 F. Calculate fc ≈ 1591.55 Hz.

Impedance Matching in Speaker Systems

Example:

Calculate total impedance of two speakers in parallel, Z1 = 4 Ω and Z2 = 8 Ω.

Result: Ztotal ≈ 2.67 Ω.
